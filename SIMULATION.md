# Are We in a Simulation?

The **simulation hypothesis** proposes that our reality may be a computer simulation
run by a more advanced civilization. Here are the key arguments:

## The Trilemma (Nick Bostrom, 2003)

At least one of the following must be true:

1. **Extinction** – Almost all civilizations go extinct before reaching the
   technological maturity needed to create realistic simulations.
2. **Disinterest** – Almost all technologically mature civilizations choose not
   to run simulations of their predecessors.
3. **We are simulated** – We are almost certainly living in a computer simulation.

If (1) and (2) are both false, the sheer number of simulated minds would vastly
outnumber biological ones, making it statistically likely that we are simulated.

## Evidence Sometimes Cited

- **Quantum discreteness** – Space, time, and energy appear to come in discrete
  units (Planck length, Planck time), resembling a pixelated rendering.
- **Speed-of-light limit** – An absolute maximum information transfer rate is
  consistent with a bandwidth-limited simulation.
- **Fine-tuned constants** – The physical constants of the universe are
  extraordinarily well-tuned for complexity; a simulator could simply have chosen
  those values.
- **Mathematical universe** – The unreasonable effectiveness of mathematics in
  describing nature suggests reality may itself be mathematical/computational.

## Counter-arguments

- Running a faithful simulation of a universe is computationally infeasible with
  any plausible physical substrate.
- The hypothesis is unfalsifiable in its strong form, placing it outside
  conventional science.
- Even if true, it has no practical consequences for how we should act.

## Conclusion

We do not currently have definitive proof either way. The simulation hypothesis
remains a fascinating philosophical thought experiment rather than an established
scientific theory. Whether simulated or not, the observable universe follows
consistent, discoverable laws — and that is what matters for science and
engineering.
